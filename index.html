Edo Roth edoroth@cis.upenn.edu

Hello and welcome! I'm a fifth-year PhD student in the Department of Computer and Information Science at the University of Pennsylvania. My advisor is Andreas Haeberlen. I am on the industry job market this year!

My research focuses on privacy and security in data analytics systems, particularly in large-scale distributed ones. I work on differential privacy, applied cryptography, federated learning, and distributed systems.

In general, I'm motivated to study and build privacy-protecting tools that lead to greater freedom of expression, and more broadly to develop technology for social good through accountability and transparency. I'm particularly curious about whether (and how) privacy-preserving technology can help enhance democracy and decrease inequality.

I grew up in Champaign-Urbana, Illinois, and completed my bachelor studies in Computer Science at Columbia University.

You can find me lurking on Twitter, GitHub, or look at books I've read: 2020, 2021.
